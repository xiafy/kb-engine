# Solid Tumor General Rules — Oncology Solid

> Cross-indication general rules. Indication-specific content: see indications/.

---

## Trial Design

### Pan-tumor Basket Trial Testing Strategy

IF design == "pan-tumor basket trial": → Accept multiple CLIA-certified testing methods (NGS/FISH/RT-PCR); do not restrict to a single platform.

### Pediatric Patient Inclusion Strategy

Molecularly targeted drugs should incorporate pediatric patients early: patients ≥12 years old may be enrolled directly in adult trials; patients <12 years require a separate PK/safety cohort. Long-term safety must cover growth and development, cognition, reproductive safety, and secondary malignancies.

### Non-cytotoxic Open-label inv-PFS Exemption

⚠️ Exemption criteria: For open-label trials of non-cytotoxic drugs (e.g., oral endocrine therapy vs IC of ET) where both the control arm and trial arm share the same drug mechanism class and disease progression assessment is minimally subject to subjective bias, FDA may accept investigator-assessed PFS as the primary endpoint (precedent: EMBER-3/Inluriyo).

### Protocol 11-Section Standard Structure
All new oncology protocols must follow the 11-section structure: (1) Protocol Summary (Synopsis + Schema + SoA); (2) Introduction (Rationale + Background + B/R); (3) Objectives & Endpoints (table format); (4) Study Design; (5) Study Population (I/E); (6) Study Intervention (dosing + concomitant + dose modification); (7) Discontinuation/Withdrawal; (8) Assessments & Procedures (efficacy + safety + AE + PK + biomarkers + PRO); (9) Statistical Considerations; (10) Supporting Documentation (Appendices); (11) References.
[source: kb/methods/sop/protocol-writing-sop.md §一]

### Open-Label Design Lessons (EMBER-3)
In open-label oncology RCTs with IC control: (1) Investigator-assessed PFS can serve as primary if non-cytotoxic and minimal subjective bias; (2) BICR must always be included as secondary/sensitivity regardless; (3) PRO assessment timing must precede clinical assessments to avoid bias; (4) Concomitant medication restrictions must account for open-label knowledge of treatment assignment.
[source: kb/oncology/open-label-oncology-trial-design-lessons-ember3.md]

### Dose Modification Tables by Toxicity Type
Oncology protocols must include separate dose modification tables for: hematologic toxicity, non-hematologic toxicity, hepatotoxicity, and drug-specific toxicities (e.g., diarrhea, QT prolongation). Each table must cover CTCAE Grade 1-4 with specific actions (continue/hold/reduce/discontinue) and re-escalation criteria if applicable. Number of dose reduction levels: typically 2 (starting dose → level -1 → discontinue).
[source: kb/methods/sop/protocol-writing-sop.md §四]
