# Question 020: Dordaviprone (Modeyso)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Dordaviprone
- **Trade Name**: Modeyso
- **Sponsor**: Chimerix, Inc.
- **Code Name**: ONC201
- **MOA**: Dual mechanism: (1) protease activator of mitochondrial caseinolytic protease P (ClpP), activating the integrated stress response and inducing apoptosis; (2) dopamine receptor D2 (DRD2) antagonist; alters mitochondrial metabolism and restores histone H3 K27 trimethylation (H3 K27me3) in H3 K27M-mutant glioma models; new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Protease activator (ClpP agonist) / DRD2 antagonist
- **Formulation**: Capsules, 125 mg

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adult and pediatric patients 1 year of age and older with diffuse midline glioma (DMG) harboring an H3 K27M mutation with progressive disease following prior therapy.

## Target Population

- Adults and pediatric patients ≥1 year of age (weighing ≥10 kg)
- Histologically or molecularly confirmed diffuse midline glioma harboring an H3 K27M mutation (includes DIPG and non-DIPG midline locations: thalamus, brainstem, spinal cord)
- Progressive disease following prior therapy (at minimum, surgical resection and/or radiotherapy)
- ≥90 days post-radiation therapy at study entry
- Karnofsky/Lansky Performance Status ≥60
- Stable or decreasing corticosteroid use

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-radiation therapy, recurrent/progressive disease); no systemic therapy options were previously approved for this disease

## Disease Background

- Diffuse midline glioma (DMG) harboring an H3 K27M mutation is a rare, aggressive, pediatric-type diffuse high-grade glioma (WHO Grade 4) molecularly defined for the first time in 2016; the H3 K27M mutation causes a global reduction in histone H3 K27 trimethylation (H3 K27me3), disrupting normal epigenetic regulation and activating oncogenic pathways.
- Estimated annual incidence in the United States is approximately 2,000 patients; CBTRUS data reports ~0.06 per 100,000 (approximately 200 patients/year by strict registry definition, likely an underestimate); designated as a rare disease.
- DMG predominantly affects children and young adults: median age at initial diagnosis is 11 years (range: 1–82 years); H3 K27M mutations are present in 80% of pediatric high-grade gliomas, 75% of thalamic tumors, ~54% of brainstem tumors, 55% of spinal cord tumors, and 24% of all pediatric gliomas.
- Median overall survival from initial diagnosis is 10–15 months; 2-year OS ranges from 5–20% depending on cohort; after disease progression following radiation, median OS is typically only 4–6 months; some studies suggest pediatric patients have shorter OS than adult patients.
- Gliomas constitute approximately half of all brain tumors in patients under 14 years of age and are the leading cause of death due to a brain tumor in pediatric patients; diffuse intrinsic pontine glioma (DIPG) represents the most common subset of pediatric H3 K27M DMG, with ~80% of DIPG cases now reclassified as DMG H3 K27M-mutant.

## Current Standard of Care

- **Frontline**:
  - Surgical resection (if feasible; typically limited due to midline location making gross total resection infeasible)
  - Radiotherapy: only established systemic therapy providing transient benefit; standard treatment post-surgery or biopsy; median PFS from initial diagnosis only 7–9 months despite radiation
- **Post-radiation / recurrent setting**:
  - No pharmacologic agents were approved specifically for H3 K27M-mutant DMG prior to dordaviprone approval
  - H3 K27M does not co-occur in gliomas with targetable genomic alterations (IDH, BRAF), precluding use of targeted agents
  - Chemotherapy (e.g., temozolomide): has not demonstrated benefit in this population
  - NCCN guidelines recommend clinical trial enrollment as preferred option for recurrent high-grade glioma; other options include re-irradiation, chemotherapy, targeted therapy based on molecular composition, and palliative/best supportive care
  - Objective responses to systemic therapies with standardized criteria and adequate washout have not been reported for recurrent H3 K27M-mutant DMG

## Unmet Medical Need

Prior to dordaviprone approval, there were no pharmacologic agents approved specifically for the treatment of H3 K27M-mutant diffuse midline glioma. This rare, rapidly fatal disease affects primarily children and young adults and has no available systemic therapy in the recurrent setting. Median OS after progression is typically only 4–6 months, and spontaneous tumor regression is not observed. The absence of effective therapies in the recurrent setting and the high morbidity and mortality of this disease represent a substantial and urgent unmet medical need for novel pharmacologic agents providing clinical benefit.
