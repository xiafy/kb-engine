# Question 011: Toripalimab-tpzi (Loqtorzi)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Toripalimab-tpzi
- **Trade Name**: LOQTORZI
- **Sponsor**: Coherus BioSciences, Inc. (originally developed by Junshi Biosciences)
- **MOA**: IgG4 monoclonal antibody directed against programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1); blocks PD-1/PD-L1 interaction to restore T-cell anti-tumor activity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1) blocking antibody
- **Formulation**: 240 mg/6 mL (40 mg/mL) solution in single-dose vial
- **Dosing**:
  - In combination with cisplatin and gemcitabine (1L): 240 mg IV Q3W until disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, or up to 24 months
  - As single agent (2L+): 3 mg/kg IV Q2W until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity

## Proposed Indication

**Indication 1**: Toripalimab in combination with cisplatin and gemcitabine, for the first-line treatment of adults with metastatic or with recurrent, locally advanced nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC).

**Indication 2**: Toripalimab as a single agent, for the treatment of adults with recurrent unresectable or metastatic NPC with disease progression on or after a platinum-containing chemotherapy.

## Target Population

**Indication 1 (1L)**:
- Adults with metastatic or recurrent, locally advanced NPC
- No prior systemic chemotherapy for recurrent or metastatic disease

**Indication 2 (2L+)**:
- Adults with recurrent unresectable or metastatic NPC
- Prior platinum-containing chemotherapy (for recurrent/metastatic disease, or disease progression within 6 months of completing platinum-based neoadjuvant/adjuvant/definitive chemoradiation for locally advanced disease)

## Line of Therapy

- Indication 1: First-line (metastatic/recurrent locally advanced NPC)
- Indication 2: Second-line or later (after platinum-containing chemotherapy)

## Disease Background

- There were 133,354 new cases of nasopharyngeal carcinoma (NPC) diagnosed globally in 2020, and approximately 80,000 people died from NPC in 2020.
- More than 75% of global NPC cases occur in South and East Asia; NPC is rare in the United States with less than 1 case per 100,000 people per year (compared with >10 cases per 100,000 in endemic regions of Southern China).
- NPC has three major WHO subtypes: non-keratinizing undifferentiated (predominant in endemic regions: ~95% in Southern China, ~63% in the US), non-keratinizing differentiated, and keratinizing squamous cell carcinoma (more common in the US: ~25% vs. ~2% in Southern China).
- The prognosis for patients with locally recurrent, unresectable, or metastatic NPC is poor, with a median OS of 21 to 29 months; there were no FDA-approved therapies for NPC (as either first- or later-line treatment) at the time of submission.
- Standard first-line chemotherapy with cisplatin plus gemcitabine demonstrated median PFS of 7.0 months and median OS of 29.1 months; response to second-line therapy after platinum failure is limited.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L (recurrent/metastatic NPC)**: Cisplatin + gemcitabine (standard of care at time of study design; no approved PD-1/PD-L1 therapy for NPC prior to toripalimab approval)
- **2L+ (after platinum failure)**:
  - No well-established standard of care; no FDA-approved therapies
  - Single-agent regimens based on small, single-arm trials only
  - Options considered: capecitabine, paclitaxel, docetaxel, gemcitabine, vinorelbine (limited clinical data)
  - Clinical trial participation strongly preferred
- Treatment of recurrent, unresectable, and metastatic NPC represents an unmet medical need given the limited treatment options and poor prognosis

## Unmet Medical Need

There were no FDA-approved therapies for NPC in any line of therapy at the time of submission. Standard first-line cisplatin/gemcitabine is palliative with limited OS benefit. After platinum failure, there is no well-established standard of care and no approved second-line therapy; usual treatment relied on single-agent regimens with limited efficacy data from small single-arm trials. Given the limited treatment options, poor prognosis (median OS of 21–29 months in the first-line metastatic setting), and absence of approved therapies, treatment of recurrent, unresectable, and metastatic NPC represented a clear unmet medical need.
