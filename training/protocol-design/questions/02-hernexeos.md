# Question 002: Zongertinib (Hernexeos)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Zongertinib
- **Trade Name**: Hernexeos
- **Sponsor**: Boehringer Ingelheim Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
- **Code Name**: BI 1810631
- **MOA**: Small molecule kinase inhibitor of HER2 (ERBB2), including wild-type HER2 and HER2 activating mutations; new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Kinase inhibitor
- **Formulation**: 60 mg tablet

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) whose tumors have HER2 (ERBB2) tyrosine kinase domain activating mutations, as detected by an FDA-approved test, and who have received prior systemic therapy.

## Target Population

- Adults with unresectable or metastatic non-squamous NSCLC
- Tumors with HER2 (ERBB2) tyrosine kinase domain (TKD) activating mutations (primarily Exon 20 mutations), as detected by an FDA-approved test
- Must have received at least one prior line of systemic therapy (including platinum-based chemotherapy)

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-platinum-based chemotherapy, with or without prior anti-PD-(L)1 antibody)

## Disease Background

- Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer-related death in the United States, with approximately 226,650 new cases and 124,730 deaths expected in 2025; NSCLC accounts for 85% of lung cancers.
- Approximately 44–48% of patients with NSCLC present with stage IV/metastatic disease at diagnosis; the 5-year survival for patients with clinical stage IV disease is less than 5%.
- HER2 (ERBB2) mutations occur in approximately 2% of all NSCLC and are primarily activating mutations involving Exon 20 of the HER2 (ERBB2) gene.
- Patients with HER2-mutated NSCLC are more likely to be female, non-smokers, and have tumors with adenocarcinoma histology; brain metastases are reported in up to 47% of these patients.
- Median survival in patients with metastatic HER2-mutated NSCLC is 17 to 23 months.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L**: Platinum-based doublet chemotherapy with or without anti-PD-(L)1 antibody (e.g., pembrolizumab + carboplatin/pemetrexed); there are no products targeting HER2-mutated NSCLC under traditional FDA approval
- **2L+ (HER2-unselected options)**:
  - Single agent chemotherapy (docetaxel, ORR 10–15%)
  - Combination chemotherapy (docetaxel + ramucirumab, ORR ~23%)
  - Anti-PD-(L)1 monotherapy for anti-PD-(L)1 naïve patients (ORR 15–20%, median DOR 16–17 months)
- **2L+ (HER2-directed, accelerated approval)**:
  - Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (T-DXd), a HER2-directed ADC granted accelerated approval August 2022 for NSCLC with activating HER2 mutations (ORR 58%, median DOR 8.7 months)

## Unmet Medical Need

There are no therapies with traditional approval specifically for patients with HER2-mutated NSCLC whose disease has progressed after standard first-line therapy. Standard treatment options provide limited efficacy with ORRs of 10–23% for chemotherapy-based regimens. Metastatic HER2-mutated NSCLC remains a fatal, incurable disease, and an unmet need exists for additional systemic therapies for patients whose disease has progressed after first-line therapy, including after treatment with a HER2-directed ADC.
