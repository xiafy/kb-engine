# Question 017: Imetelstat (Rytelo)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Imetelstat
- **Trade Name**: Rytelo
- **Sponsor**: Geron Corporation
- **Code Name**: GRN163L, JNJ-63935937
- **MOA**: Covalently-lipidated 13-mer oligonucleotide that binds to the template region of the RNA component of human telomerase (hTR), inhibits telomerase enzymatic activity and prevents telomere binding; new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Telomerase inhibitor
- **Formulation**: Lyophilized powder (47 mg, 188 mg) for intravenous injection

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adult patients with low- to intermediate-1 risk myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS) with transfusion-dependent anemia requiring 4 or more red blood cell units over 8 weeks who have not responded to or have lost response to or are ineligible for erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESA).

## Target Population

- Adults with IPSS low- or intermediate-1 risk MDS
- Transfusion-dependent anemia requiring ≥4 RBC units over 8 weeks
- Failed to respond, lost response to, or ineligible for ESA treatment
- No restriction on ring sideroblast or del(5q) status

## Line of Therapy

Second-line (post-ESA failure or ESA-ineligible); following frontline ESA-based therapy

## Disease Background

- Myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS) are a heterogeneous group of clonal hematopoietic stem cell disorders characterized by ineffective hematopoiesis, bone marrow dysplasia, peripheral cytopenias, and variable risk of transformation to acute myeloid leukemia (AML).
- MDS is one of the most common hematologic malignancies in older adults, with a median age at diagnosis >70 years; incidence in the United States is estimated at 3.8 per 100,000 (2019), rising to 39.7 per 100,000 among those aged ≥75 years.
- Lower-risk (LR) MDS (IPSS low and intermediate-1) carries a median survival of 5.7 years (low risk) and 3.5 years (intermediate-1 risk); approximately 40% of LR MDS patients develop anemia and become transfusion dependent.
- Chronic transfusion-dependent anemia in LR MDS is associated with fatigue, impaired quality of life (QoL), iron overload, higher cardiovascular risk, and shortened overall survival; higher RBC transfusion rate correlates with decreased PFS and OS.
- Shorter telomere length and high telomerase activity have been reported in LR MDS, suggesting telomerase is a relevant therapeutic target; patients with LR MDS who fail ESA have a median survival of ~36.7 months versus 54.3 months for longer ESA responders.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L (ESA-eligible)**:
  - Erythropoiesis-stimulating agents (ESAs, e.g., epoetin alfa, darbepoetin alfa): established first-line treatment for transfusion-dependent anemia in LR MDS (not FDA-approved for MDS but widely used)
  - Luspatercept (Reblozyl): FDA-approved for anemia in ESA-naïve IPSS-R very low- to intermediate-risk MDS requiring regular RBC transfusions; also approved post-ESA failure in MDS with ringed sideroblasts (MDS-RS+) or MDS/MPN-RS-T
- **1L (del(5q) subset)**:
  - Lenalidomide: approved for transfusion-dependent anemia in LR MDS with deletion 5q; not approved in non-del(5q) patients
- **Post-ESA failure (unmet need)**:
  - Luspatercept: restricted to MDS-RS+ (approximately 15–30% of LR MDS); 8-week TI rate 38%, median response duration 30.6 weeks
  - Hypomethylating agents (azacitidine, decitabine, decitabine-cedazuridine): primarily used in higher-risk MDS; not recommended as standard of care in LR MDS; limited and inconsistent benefit in lower-risk disease
  - Hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT): only potentially curative option, but associated with lower survival in LR MDS patients; not recommended for lower-risk disease

## Unmet Medical Need

There is no therapy approved for the treatment of transfusion-dependent anemia in LR MDS patients (RS- and RS+, non-del(5q)) who have failed to respond, lost response to, or are ineligible for ESA that has shown sustained and durable activity in a broad patient population. The current treatment options after ESA failure are inadequate: luspatercept is restricted to MDS-RS+ patients, lenalidomide is restricted to del(5q) disease, and HMAs have not demonstrated consistent benefit or OS improvement in LR MDS. This leaves a large proportion of transfusion-dependent LR MDS patients with no effective approved therapy, representing a high unmet medical need.
