# Question 005: Zolbetuximab-clzb (Vyloy)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Zolbetuximab-clzb
- **Trade Name**: Vyloy
- **Sponsor**: Astellas Pharma US, Inc.
- **Code Name**: IMAB362
- **MOA**: Chimeric (mouse/human) IgG1 antibody directed against claudin 18 isoform 2 (CLDN18.2); depletes CLDN18.2-positive cells via antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC) and complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC)
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Claudin 18.2-directed cytolytic antibody
- **Formulation**: 100 mg lyophilized powder in single-dose vials for intravenous infusion

## Proposed Indication

In combination with fluoropyrimidine- and platinum-containing chemotherapy for the first-line treatment of adults with locally advanced unresectable or metastatic HER2-negative gastric or gastroesophageal junction (GEJ) adenocarcinoma whose tumors are claudin (CLDN) 18.2 positive as determined by an FDA-approved test.

## Target Population

- Adults with locally advanced unresectable or metastatic gastric or GEJ adenocarcinoma
- HER2-negative tumors
- CLDN18.2 positive: ≥75% of tumor cells demonstrating moderate to strong membranous CLDN18 staining (IHC 2+ or 3+) as determined by the VENTANA CLDN18 (43-14A) RxDx Assay
- No prior systemic treatment for advanced/metastatic disease
- No complete or partial gastric outlet syndrome; no history of CNS metastases

## Line of Therapy

First-line (treatment-naïve for locally advanced or metastatic disease)

## Disease Background

- Gastric cancer is the fifth most common cancer and the fourth leading cause of cancer death worldwide; in 2020, there were an estimated 1.1 million new cases and approximately 770,000 deaths worldwide.
- In the U.S. in 2023, an estimated 26,500 new cases and 11,130 deaths from gastric cancer were expected; the average age at diagnosis is 68–69 years, and incidence is approximately 2-fold higher in men than women.
- Approximately 35% of patients diagnosed with gastric cancer in the U.S. have metastatic disease; the 5-year survival rate for metastatic gastric cancer is approximately 6%.
- Incidence is markedly higher in Eastern Asia (primarily China), where approximately 60% of global cases occur; GEJ carcinoma is showing the fastest growing increase in incidence in Western populations, driven by increasing rates of obesity and gastroesophageal reflux disease.
- CLDN18.2 expression varies by population and assay cutoff; approximately 27–52% of gastric cancers express CLDN18.2 at moderate to strong levels; no targeted therapy had been approved specifically for CLDN18.2-positive tumors at the time of BLA submission.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L (HER2-negative)**:
  - Fluoropyrimidine + platinum-based chemotherapy (e.g., mFOLFOX6 or CAPOX) as backbone
  - Nivolumab (anti-PD-1) + fluoropyrimidine- and platinum-containing chemotherapy: FDA approved April 2021 (CHECKMATE-649); recommended for patients with PD-L1 CPS ≥5 (mOS 14.4 vs 11.1 months; HR 0.71)
  - Pembrolizumab + fluoropyrimidine- and platinum-containing chemotherapy: FDA approved November 2023 (KEYNOTE-859); for HER2-negative gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma (mOS benefit in CPS ≥1 and all-comer populations)
  - No targeted therapy specifically approved for CLDN18.2-positive tumors prior to zolbetuximab
- **2L**:
  - Ramucirumab + paclitaxel (RAINBOW; mOS 9.6 vs 7.4 months)
  - Ramucirumab monotherapy (REGARD; mOS 5.2 vs 3.8 months)
  - Single-agent chemotherapy (irinotecan, docetaxel)
  - Trifluridine/tipiracil (TAS-102) for ≥2 prior lines

## Unmet Medical Need

Despite advances with immune checkpoint inhibitors, expected median survival for advanced or metastatic HER2-negative gastric/GEJ cancer with currently available standard of care is just under 1 year. There are no agents approved specifically for the treatment of CLDN18.2-positive tumors. The genetic complexity and heterogeneity of gastric/GEJ adenocarcinoma has limited treatment advances; more than 80% of patients have HER2-negative disease with fewer targeted options, and there is a clear need for precision medicine approaches using biomarker-selected populations. Fluoropyrimidine- and platinum-based chemotherapy with immune checkpoint inhibitors improves survival but does not represent a curative option, and outcomes for the CLDN18.2-positive subgroup needed further investigation with a targeted agent.
