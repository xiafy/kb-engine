# Question 001: Imlunestrant (Inluriyo)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Imlunestrant
- **Trade Name**: Inluriyo
- **Sponsor**: Eli Lilly and Company
- **Code Name**: LY3484356
- **MOA**: Orally bioavailable estrogen receptor (ER) antagonist that binds to ERα; new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Estrogen receptor antagonist
- **Formulation**: 200 mg oral tablets

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adults with ER-positive, HER2-negative, ESR1-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer, previously treated with an endocrine-based regimen.

## Target Population

- Adults with estrogen receptor (ER)-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative, ESR1-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer
- Disease progression following at least one line of endocrine therapy
- Biomarker requirement: ESR1 mutation (acquired activating missense mutations or in-frame deletions in the ligand-binding domain of ESR1, estimated in ~10-50% of patients exposed to aromatase inhibitors for metastatic breast cancer)

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-endocrine therapy progression, with or without prior CDK4/6 inhibitor)

## Disease Background

- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide. In the U.S., an estimated 316,950 new cases and 42,170 deaths in 2025.
- ~70% of breast cancers are ER-positive, HER2-negative.
- Advanced/metastatic ER+/HER2- breast cancer is incurable with a median OS of 3-5 years.
- Acquired endocrine resistance, particularly to aromatase inhibitors, can occur due to ESR1 mutations.
- Male patients can also develop breast cancer (<1%), often presenting at a higher stage.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L**: CDK4/6 inhibitor (abemaciclib, palbociclib, ribociclib) + endocrine therapy (AI or fulvestrant)
- **2L+**: Depends on prior therapy and tumor mutations:
  - Endocrine monotherapy: fulvestrant, AI, tamoxifen
  - Everolimus + endocrine therapy
  - Targeted combinations: alpelisib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated), capivasertib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN-altered), PARP inhibitors (BRCA-mutated)
  - Elacestrant (oral SERD, approved Jan 2023 for ESR1-mutated, post-1L endocrine therapy)
  - Inavolisib + palbociclib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated, approved 2024)
  - Chemotherapy for endocrine-refractory disease

## Unmet Medical Need

There remains an unmet need for additional therapies to improve clinical outcomes in patients with ER+, HER2-, ESR1-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer. Elacestrant is the only approved oral SERD for this population, and the ideal sequencing of therapies remains unknown.
