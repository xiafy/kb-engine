# Question 003: Sevabertinib (Hyrnuo)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Sevabertinib
- **Trade Name**: HYRNUO
- **Sponsor**: Bayer HealthCare Pharmaceuticals Inc.
- **Code Name**: BAY 2927088
- **MOA**: Reversible oral small molecule kinase inhibitor of HER2 (ERBB2), including HER2 exon 20 insertion mutations and HER2 point mutations; also exhibits activity against wild-type EGFR and certain EGFR mutants
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Kinase inhibitor
- **Formulation**: Tablets

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adult patients with locally advanced or metastatic non-squamous non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) whose tumors have HER2 (ERBB2) tyrosine kinase domain activating mutations, as detected by an FDA-approved test, and who have received prior systemic therapy.

## Target Population

- Adults with locally advanced or metastatic non-squamous NSCLC
- Tumors with HER2 (ERBB2) tyrosine kinase domain (TKD) activating mutations, as detected by an FDA-approved test
- Must have received at least one prior line of systemic therapy
- Two populations: anti-HER2 therapy-naïve (Group D) and patients who have progressed on prior anti-HER2 directed therapy (Group E)

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-platinum-based chemotherapy, with or without prior anti-PD-(L)1 antibody; includes post-HER2-directed ADC setting)

## Disease Background

- Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer-related death in the United States, with an estimated 234,580 new cases and 125,070 deaths in 2024; NSCLC is the most common type, accounting for ≥85% of lung cancer cases.
- Nearly 75% of NSCLC patients are diagnosed at an advanced stage; the overall 5-year survival rate of NSCLC is approximately 16%, while in advanced NSCLC it ranges from 2.8% to 14.6%.
- HER2 (ERBB2) mutations occur in approximately 2% of all NSCLC cases, with the vast majority (96%) being HER2 exon 20 insertions; HER2 activating mutations are associated with female sex, Asian ethnicity, non-smoking status, adenocarcinoma histology, and a median age of approximately 60 years.
- Patients with HER2-mutant NSCLC have a worse prognosis (median OS 1.6 to 1.9 years from diagnosis) than NSCLC with EGFR or ALK mutations, partially due to lack of effective targeted therapies.
- Brain metastases are reported in up to 47% of patients with HER2-mutated NSCLC.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L**: Platinum-based doublet chemotherapy with or without anti-PD-(L)1 antibody (ORR ~36–37%, median PFS 5.1–7.1 months); no targeted therapy has received full approval specifically for HER2-mutated NSCLC
- **2L+ (HER2-unselected options)**:
  - Single agent docetaxel (ORR 6–23%)
  - Docetaxel + ramucirumab (ORR ~23%, 95% CI: 20, 26)
  - Anti-PD-L1 monotherapy for anti-PD-L1 naïve patients (ORR 14–20%)
- **2L+ (HER2-directed, accelerated approval)**:
  - Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (T-DXd), granted accelerated approval August 2022 for HER2-mutated NSCLC (ORR 57.7% at interim analysis, N=52; confirmed ORR 49% at primary analysis, N=102)
  - Zongertinib (Hernexeos), granted accelerated approval August 8, 2025, for unresectable or metastatic non-squamous NSCLC with HER2 (ERBB2) TKD activating mutations

## Unmet Medical Need

Despite recent advances with HER2-directed ADCs (T-DXd) and TKIs (zongertinib), there remains an urgent need for more effective and better tolerated agents that can improve response rates and response durability in patients with HER2-activating mutations in NSCLC. T-DXd is associated with safety concerns including adjudicated drug-related ILD in 12.9% of patients. No therapy has traditional approval for this population, and patients who progress after T-DXd have no approved HER2-directed options. An unmet need exists for additional systemic therapies for patients whose disease has progressed after first-line therapy, including post-HER2-directed ADC settings.
