# Question 006: Selpercatinib (Retevmo)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Selpercatinib
- **Trade Name**: Retevmo
- **Sponsor**: Loxo Oncology, Inc. (wholly owned subsidiary of Eli Lilly and Company)
- **Code Name**: LOXO-292
- **MOA**: Selective inhibitor of the RET receptor tyrosine kinase as well as VEGFR1 and VEGFR3; targets oncogenic RET gene fusions and activating point mutations; new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Kinase inhibitor
- **Formulation**: Capsule

## Proposed Indication

Three indications simultaneously:
1. Treatment of adult patients with metastatic RET fusion-positive non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC)
2. Treatment of patients ≥12 years old with advanced or metastatic RET-mutant medullary thyroid cancer (MTC) who require systemic therapy
3. Treatment of patients ≥12 years old with advanced RET fusion-positive thyroid cancer who require systemic therapy and who are radioactive iodine-refractory (if radioactive iodine is appropriate)

## Target Population

**NSCLC population:**
- Adults with metastatic RET fusion-positive NSCLC
- RET fusion detected by a CLIA-certified (or equivalent) test
- Measurable disease per RECIST 1.1
- Includes both treatment-naïve and platinum-treated populations

**MTC population:**
- Patients ≥12 years old with advanced or metastatic RET mutation-positive MTC
- Includes both cabozantinib/vandetanib-naïve and previously-treated patients

**Thyroid cancer population:**
- Patients ≥12 years old with advanced RET fusion-positive thyroid cancer
- Radioactive iodine-refractory (where RAI is appropriate based on histology)

## Line of Therapy

- NSCLC: First-line and second-line or later (line-agnostic for RET fusion-positive NSCLC)
- MTC: First-line and post-cabozantinib/vandetanib
- Thyroid cancer: Post-RAI refractory setting

## Disease Background

- Lung cancer: There were an estimated 228,150 new cases and 142,670 deaths from lung and bronchial cancers in the U.S. in 2019; NSCLC accounts for 80% of lung cancers; 5-year survival for stage IV disease is less than 5%; RET gene fusions are identified in 1–2% of patients with NSCLC and are associated with younger age (≤60 years) and minimal/no prior tobacco exposure.
- MTC: Medullary thyroid cancer accounts for 3–4% of all thyroid cancers; approximately 50% of sporadic cases carry a somatic RET mutation and at least 90% of familial cases (MEN2A, MEN2B, familial MTC) have an identifiable germline mutation; 5-year overall survival is 86%, but 5-year relative survival for stage IV disease is approximately 28%.
- RET fusion-positive thyroid cancer: An estimated 52,890 new cases and 2,180 deaths from thyroid cancer were estimated for the U.S. in 2020; RET rearrangements are identified in 5–10% of papillary thyroid carcinomas; up to 30% of DTC patients may have disease recurrence; in patients with distant metastases, 5-year survival is 50%; anaplastic thyroid cancer has a median survival of 5 months and 1-year survival of 20%.

## Current Standard of Care

**RET fusion-positive NSCLC:**
- **1L (unselected NSCLC)**: Platinum-based doublet chemotherapy ± anti-PD-(L)1 antibody (ORR 48–58% for pembrolizumab + chemo or atezolizumab + bevacizumab + chemo in non-squamous); no approved RET-directed therapies
- **2L (unselected NSCLC)**: Single agent chemotherapy (docetaxel, pemetrexed; ORR ~5–25%), anti-PD-(L)1 monotherapy, ramucirumab + docetaxel (maximum ORR 23%)
- Emerging data suggest limited efficacy (ORR 6%, median PFS 2.1–3.4 months) for immune checkpoint inhibitors in RET fusion-positive NSCLC specifically

**RET mutation-positive MTC:**
- Cabozantinib (ORR 28% in prior pivotal study) — multi-kinase inhibitor including RET
- Vandetanib (ORR 45% in prior pivotal study) — multi-kinase inhibitor including RET
- No approved therapies specifically for RET-mutant MTC at the time of NDA submission

**RET fusion-positive thyroid cancer:**
- Radioactive iodine (if appropriate based on histology)
- Lenvatinib (ORR 65% in RAI-refractory DTC; not RET-specific)
- Sorafenib (ORR 12% in RAI-refractory DTC; not RET-specific)
- Doxorubicin (approved for thyroid carcinoma)
- No approved therapies specifically for RET fusion-positive thyroid cancer or in the second-line post-RAI setting

## Unmet Medical Need

There are no FDA-approved treatments specifically for RET fusion-positive or mutation-positive cancers; patients are treated with the standard of care for their tumor type regardless of RET alteration. For RET fusion-positive NSCLC, available non-RET-directed therapies provide ORRs of only 10–23% in the second-line setting. For RET-mutant MTC, patients who have progressed on cabozantinib/vandetanib have no approved options. For RET fusion-positive RAI-refractory thyroid cancer, available therapies are not RET-directed and have variable efficacy. Tumor genomic profiling is an emerging standard for actionable biomarkers, reinforcing the public health need for approved RET-directed agents.
