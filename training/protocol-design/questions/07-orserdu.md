# Question 007: Elacestrant (Orserdu)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Elacestrant
- **Trade Name**: Orserdu
- **Sponsor**: Stemline Therapeutics, Inc.
- **Code Name**: RAD1901
- **MOA**: Orally bioavailable estrogen receptor (ER) antagonist; selective estrogen receptor degrader (SERD); new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Estrogen receptor antagonist
- **Formulation**: 345 mg and 86 mg oral tablets

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of postmenopausal women or adult men with estrogen receptor (ER)-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative, ESR1-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer with disease progression following at least one line of endocrine therapy.

## Target Population

- Postmenopausal women or adult men with ER-positive, HER2-negative, ESR1-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer
- Disease progression following at least one line of endocrine therapy
- Biomarker requirement: ESR1 mutations (activating missense mutations in the ligand-binding domain of ESR1), estimated to develop in approximately 20–40% of patients exposed to aromatase inhibitors in the metastatic setting

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-progression on at least one endocrine therapy including a CDK4/6 inhibitor)

## Disease Background

- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide. In the U.S., an estimated 297,790 new cases of breast cancer and 43,170 deaths were estimated for 2023.
- Approximately 70% of patients with breast cancer have ER-positive, HER2-negative disease.
- Advanced or metastatic ER-positive, HER2-negative breast cancer is incurable; the goal of treatment is palliative.
- By January 2017, an estimated 154,794 women in the U.S. were living with metastatic breast cancer, with 3 in 4 initially diagnosed with stage I–III disease who later progressed.
- Acquired resistance to aromatase inhibitors (AIs) via ESR1 mutations occurs in approximately 20–40% of patients with metastatic ER+/HER2− breast cancer exposed to AIs; no FDA-approved therapies specifically targeted ESR1-mutated breast cancer prior to elacestrant.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L**: CDK4/6 inhibitor (abemaciclib, palbociclib, or ribociclib) + endocrine therapy (AI or fulvestrant)
- **2L+** (post-CDK4/6 inhibitor progression):
  - Endocrine monotherapy: fulvestrant (only approved SERD at the time, administered IM), AI, or tamoxifen
  - Everolimus + endocrine therapy (exemestane or fulvestrant)
  - Alpelisib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated tumors)
  - Chemotherapy: capecitabine, eribulin, ixabepilone, paclitaxel protein-bound, gemcitabine
  - Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan (HER2-low disease)
- No FDA-approved therapies specifically for ESR1-mutated ER+/HER2− breast cancer existed prior to elacestrant approval
- Fulvestrant monotherapy post-CDK4/6 inhibitor shows limited efficacy (median PFS ~1.9–2.2 months in recent studies)

## Unmet Medical Need

There is an unmet medical need to improve clinical outcomes in patients with ER-positive, HER2-negative advanced or metastatic breast cancer, particularly in the ESR1-mutated subpopulation. All current treatment options are palliative, and no FDA-approved therapies specifically targeted patients with ESR1 mutations. Sequential endocrine therapy has limited activity post-CDK4/6 inhibitor, and the intramuscular route of administration of fulvestrant is a barrier for some patients. An oral SERD specifically for ESR1-mutated disease represented a significant unmet need.
