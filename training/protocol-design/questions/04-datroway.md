# Question 004: Datopotamab Deruxtecan-dlnk (Datroway)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Datopotamab deruxtecan-dlnk (Dato-DXd)
- **Trade Name**: DATROWAY
- **Sponsor**: Daiichi Sankyo, Inc.
- **MOA**: Trop2-directed antibody and topoisomerase inhibitor conjugate; contains three components: a humanized anti-Trop2 IgG1 monoclonal antibody, a topoisomerase 1 inhibitor DXd, and a cleavable linker
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Trop2-directed antibody and topoisomerase inhibitor conjugate
- **Formulation**: 100 mg lyophilized powder for intravenous infusion

## Proposed Indication

Treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic hormone receptor (HR)-positive, HER2-negative (IHC 0, IHC 1+ or IHC 2+/ISH-) breast cancer who have received prior endocrine-based therapy and chemotherapy for unresectable or metastatic disease.

## Target Population

- Adults with unresectable or metastatic HR-positive, HER2-negative (IHC 0, 1+, or 2+/ISH-) breast cancer
- Must have received prior endocrine-based therapy for unresectable or metastatic disease
- Must have received prior chemotherapy (1 or 2 lines) for unresectable or metastatic disease
- Disease must not be suitable for further endocrine therapy

## Line of Therapy

Third-line or later (post endocrine-based therapy and 1–2 prior lines of chemotherapy in the metastatic setting)

## Disease Background

- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide; in the U.S., an estimated 313,510 new cases and 42,780 deaths were expected in 2024, with an additional 2,790 new cases and 530 deaths in male patients.
- Approximately 70% of all breast cancers are of the subtype HR-positive, HER2-negative (IHC 0, 1+, or 2+/ISH-); this represents the most common breast cancer subtype.
- In early stages, HR+/HER2- breast cancer is associated with a better prognosis than other breast cancer subtypes; however, once locally advanced or metastatic, it is incurable and associated with a poor prognosis.
- Approximately 5–10% of women diagnosed with breast cancer present with metastatic disease at diagnosis, and up to 30% with early-stage non-metastatic breast cancer will develop metastatic disease.
- Median OS for patients with HR+/HER2-negative advanced/metastatic breast cancer receiving chemotherapy has been reported at less than 18–24 months in recent randomized trials.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L (metastatic)**: CDK4/6 inhibitor (palbociclib, abemaciclib, or ribociclib) in combination with endocrine therapy (aromatase inhibitor or fulvestrant)
- **2L (endocrine-based options)**:
  - Elacestrant (oral SERD) for ESR1-mutated tumors
  - Capivasertib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN-altered tumors)
  - Alpelisib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated tumors)
  - Everolimus + exemestane
  - Inavolisib + palbociclib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated, approved October 2024)
- **Post-endocrine (chemotherapy)**:
  - Sequential single-agent chemotherapy: capecitabine (often preferred as initial chemotherapy due to oral administration), eribulin, vinorelbine, gemcitabine, taxanes
  - Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan-nxki (T-DXd): approved for HER2-low (IHC 1+ or IHC 2+/ISH-) tumors following ≥1 prior chemotherapy in the metastatic setting
  - Sacituzumab govitecan-hziy (SG): approved for HR+/HER2- tumors following prior endocrine therapy and at least 2 additional prior systemic therapies in the metastatic setting

## Unmet Medical Need

Once patients with HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer are no longer deriving benefit from endocrine therapy-based regimens, treatment is palliative and all options have limited efficacy. While T-DXd (for HER2-low) and sacituzumab govitecan have more recently been approved, the activity of Dato-DXd following other approved ADCs and vice versa is unknown. There remains an unmet medical need to improve outcomes for patients who have received prior endocrine therapy and chemotherapy, particularly for those with HER2-ultralow (IHC 0) tumors who are not eligible for T-DXd, as well as patients who may prefer a less myelosuppressive option than chemotherapy.
