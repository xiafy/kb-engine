# Question 008: Capivasertib (Truqap)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Capivasertib
- **Trade Name**: Truqap
- **Sponsor**: AstraZeneca Inc.
- **Code Name**: AZD5363
- **MOA**: Small molecule inhibitor of all 3 isoforms of serine/threonine kinase AKT (AKT1, AKT2, AKT3); new molecular entity
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Kinase Inhibitor
- **Formulation**: Tablets 160 mg and 200 mg; dosing 400 mg (two 200 mg tablets) orally twice daily, 4 days on / 3 days off per week

## Proposed Indication

Capivasertib in combination with fulvestrant for the treatment of adult patients with hormone receptor (HR)-positive, human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2)-negative locally advanced or metastatic breast cancer with one or more PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN-alterations as detected by an FDA-approved test, following progression on at least one endocrine-based regimen in the metastatic setting or recurrence on or within 12 months of completing adjuvant therapy.

## Target Population

- Adults (including pre/peri-menopausal women on LHRH agonist, and men) with HR-positive, HER2-negative locally advanced or metastatic breast cancer
- Tumor biomarker: PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN alteration as detected by FoundationOne CDx (FDA-approved test)
  - Approximately 40% of advanced ER+ tumors carry activating PIK3CA mutations; ~5–10% harbor AKT-activating mutations; ~5–10% have inactivating PTEN alterations
- Progression on at least one endocrine-based regimen (including CDK4/6 inhibitor-based) in the metastatic setting

## Line of Therapy

Second-line or later (post-progression on aromatase inhibitor-based therapy in the metastatic setting)

## Disease Background

- Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women worldwide. In the U.S., an estimated 297,790 new cases and 43,170 deaths were estimated for 2023.
- Approximately 70% of breast cancers are hormone receptor (HR)-positive, HER2-negative; this subtype is the most frequent.
- Advanced or metastatic HR-positive, HER2-negative breast cancer is virtually incurable; treatment is palliative.
- Pre/perimenopausal women account for approximately 30% of breast cancer patients diagnosed globally and have shown a rising incidence of metastatic disease.
- The PIK3CA-AKT-mTOR pathway is among the most commonly activated oncogenic pathways in breast cancer and is associated with resistance to endocrine therapy, HER2-directed therapy, and cytotoxic therapy.

## Current Standard of Care

- **1L (metastatic)**: CDK4/6 inhibitor + endocrine therapy (AI or fulvestrant); combination therapy significantly improved PFS and OS versus endocrine monotherapy
- **2L+**:
  - Endocrine monotherapy: fulvestrant, AI, or tamoxifen (limited efficacy post-CDK4/6 inhibitor; median PFS approximately 1.9–2.2 months)
  - Everolimus + exemestane (approved for post-AI failure, not formally studied post-CDK4/6 inhibitor)
  - Alpelisib + fulvestrant (PIK3CA-mutated tumors; limited uptake due to hyperglycemia toxicity)
  - CDK4/6 inhibitor re-use with second endocrine agent (common U.S. practice)
  - Fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan (HER2-low tumors, after prior chemotherapy)
  - Chemotherapy (capecitabine, eribulin, ixabepilone, paclitaxel protein-bound, gemcitabine)
- All 2nd/3rd-line therapies extend survival 1–2 years; disease remains incurable

## Unmet Medical Need

There are limited options for patients with HR-positive, HER2-negative advanced or metastatic breast cancer after progression on endocrine therapy and CDK4/6 inhibitor. All treatment options are palliative. There is an unmet medical need to improve outcomes, particularly for patients whose tumors harbor PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN alterations. While alpelisib targets PIK3CA, its clinical adoption is limited by toxicity; no approved therapy existed targeting AKT1 mutations or PTEN loss.
