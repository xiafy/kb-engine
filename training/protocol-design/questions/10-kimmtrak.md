# Question 010: Tebentafusp-tebn (Kimmtrak)

## Drug Information

- **Generic Name**: Tebentafusp-tebn
- **Trade Name**: Kimmtrak
- **Sponsor**: Immunocore, LLC
- **Code Name**: IMCgp100
- **MOA**: Bispecific gp100 peptide-HLA-directed CD3 T-cell engager (ImmTAC – Immune Mobilizing Monoclonal T cell receptor Against Cancer); binds gp100 peptide presented on HLA-A*02:01 on uveal melanoma tumor cells and redirects CD3+ T cells to kill tumor cells; novel biologic mechanism
- **Pharmacologic Class**: Bispecific gp100 peptide-HLA-directed CD3 T-cell engager
- **Formulation**: Injection, Solution, 100 mcg in 0.5 mL, intravenous infusion; dosing 20 mcg on Day 1, 30 mcg on Day 8, 68 mcg on Day 15, then 68 mcg weekly via IV infusion

## Proposed Indication

For the treatment of HLA-A*02:01-positive adult patients with unresectable or metastatic uveal melanoma.

## Target Population

- HLA-A*02:01-positive adults (HLA-A*02:01 is present in ~40–50% of Caucasian patients)
- Unresectable or metastatic uveal melanoma (mUM)
- Treatment-naive in the metastatic setting (no prior systemic therapy for metastatic disease; no prior regional liver-directed therapy)
- First-line metastatic treatment

## Line of Therapy

First-line in the metastatic setting (treatment-naive for metastatic disease)

## Disease Background

- Uveal melanoma is a rare subset of melanoma accounting for less than 5% of all melanoma cases in the United States, yet it is the most frequently occurring primary intraocular malignancy in adults (approximately 85% of all primary intraocular tumors).
- The incidence of uveal melanoma is estimated at 4 to 5 cases per million per year; incidence varies by geography, race, and age, ranging from 5.3 to 10.9 cases per million.
- Despite localized disease often being treated with radiation or surgery, up to 50% of patients with uveal melanoma eventually develop systemic metastases, predominantly to the liver (~90% of patients).
- Uveal melanoma is biologically, genetically, and clinically distinct from cutaneous melanoma (CM): it harbors a low tumor mutational burden (TMB), is driven primarily by mutations in GNAQ/GNA11, and has a highly immunosuppressive tumor microenvironment—making it less sensitive to immune checkpoint inhibitors than CM.
- Once metastatic disease develops, prognosis is dismal: median OS is less than 6 months (Rantala 2019), and there had been no significant improvement in survival for patients with mUM over the past 40 years.

## Current Standard of Care

- **Non-metastatic disease**: Local treatment with radiation or surgery depending on tumor size and location
- **Metastatic/unresectable disease** (no FDA-approved therapies at time of submission):
  - NCCN 2021 recommended clinical trial participation as the preferred option
  - When trials unavailable: combination approach using liver-directed therapies (ablative procedures, embolization, hepatic perfusion, resection, or radiation) and/or systemic therapies (immune-oncology agents, cytotoxic regimens, or trametinib) for limited/symptomatic extrahepatic disease
  - Therapies approved for cutaneous melanoma have been applied off-label with limited benefit:
    - Single-agent chemotherapy (dacarbazine): median OS ~10 months
    - Anti-CTLA-4 (ipilimumab): median OS ~6–10 months
    - Anti-PD-1 (pembrolizumab): median OS ~6–10 months
    - Combination anti-CTLA-4 + anti-PD-1: numerically higher ORR but similar 1-year OS rate (~43%)
  - In a global meta-analysis of >900 patients in Phase 2 trials in mUM, median OS was approximately 9 months across all treatment modalities

## Unmet Medical Need

There were no approved therapies specifically for uveal melanoma, and no effective systemic therapy options once disease had metastasized. All available systemic treatments (approved for cutaneous melanoma) demonstrated limited benefit in mUM due to the distinct tumor biology (low TMB, immunosuppressive microenvironment). Given the poor prognosis (median OS <6 months from metastasis), there remained a significant unmet medical need for novel therapies with different mechanisms of action.
